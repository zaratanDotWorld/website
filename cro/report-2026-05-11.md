Same issue: `cta_email_chorewheel` vs `cta_email_cw`, `cta_book_consultation` vs `cta_book_consult`. Attribution is unreliable until this is fixed.

## What Is Working

- **`/houses/sage` engagement is strong.** 0.774 engagement rate, 166s avg session, 3.26 pageviews/session. People who find this page are genuinely interested ‚Äî the content is doing its job.
- **`/chorewheel/start` converts well.** 18 sessions, 0.889 engagement rate, 91s avg duration. Visitors who reach this page are primed and engaged.
- **`/chorewheel/things` dwell time is exceptional.** 541s average session duration on 13 sessions ‚Äî people spend 9 minutes here. The content is landing.
- **Instagram traffic engages at 100%.** Small volume (4‚Äì5 sessions), but every session from `ig`/`instagram.com` is engaged. The social creative is pre-qualifying visitors well.
- **`/houses/cactus` tops the engagement rate chart** at 0.900 despite only 30 sessions ‚Äî a bright spot if traffic ever grows.

## What Is Underperforming

- **Homepage engagement (0.525).** The single highest-traffic page converts fewer than half of visitors to any engaged action. Direct traffic (168 sessions, 0.446 engagement) is the dominant channel and is almost certainly landing here; the bounce rate likely exceeds 50%.
- **Sage House waitlist CTA (4.8%).** The page earns attention; the CTA does not collect it. Three `cta_waitlist_sage` events from 62 sessions with 166s avg dwell is a clear funnel failure.
- **Blog traffic bounces immediately.** `/p/three-provocations`: 0.273 engagement, 9.6s. `/p/quadratic-v-pairwise`: 0.167 engagement, 3.2s. `/p/the-story-of-chore-wheel`: 0.167 engagement, 20.7s. Organic search is finding these posts but readers are leaving before forming any intent.
- **Chore Wheel install drop-off.** 12 `cta_chorewheel_get_started` fires but only 4 `cta_install_chores` ‚Äî and only 2 each for Hearts and Things ‚Äî suggests the install flow after clicking through is unclear or blocked.
- **Event naming fragmentation.** `cta_chorewheel_get_started` (legacy, 12 events) vs `cta_cw_get_started` (canonical, 2 events) are counting separately. The true volume of a critical CTA is unknown.

## Recommendations

### 1. Fix event name fragmentation ‚Äî all Chore Wheel pages and `/about`
**Change:** Audit every CTA component that fires a GA4 event. Replace legacy names with the canonical names defined in `utils/track.js`:
- `cta_chorewheel_get_started` ‚Üí `cta_cw_get_started` (`CTA.cwGetStarted`)
- `cta_email_chorewheel` ‚Üí `cta_email_cw` (`CTA.emailCw`)
- `cta_book_consultation` ‚Üí `cta_book_consult` (`CTA.bookConsult`)
- Investigate `cta_interest_form` (not in `track.js` at all) ‚Äî either add it to `track.js` or replace with the correct canonical name.

**Expected impact:** Restores accurate attribution immediately. Currently the most-fired Chore Wheel CTA (`cta_chorewheel_get_started`, 12 events) cannot be trusted, making all downstream funnel analysis unreliable.

**Effort:** S

### 2. Strengthen the Sage House waitlist CTA ‚Äî `/houses/sage`
**Change:** The page earns 166s avg dwell and 3.26 pageviews/session but converts only 4.8% to `cta_waitlist_sage`. Two targeted fixes:
1. Add a second `cta_waitlist_sage` trigger mid-page (after the house description, before or after photos) ‚Äî not just at the top or bottom.
2. Add a 3-step "How applying works" block (Apply ‚Üí Visit ‚Üí Move in) directly above the CTA, modeled on the Outpost Club pattern documented in `spec/GROWTH.md`. This reduces perceived risk for first-time applicants.

**Expected impact:** Lift `cta_waitlist_sage` from 3 to 8‚Äì12 per month. The intent signal is already there ‚Äî the page just needs to close.

**Effort:** S
