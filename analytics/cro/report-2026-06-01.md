## What Is Underperforming

**`/p/chores-as-complex-coordination` bounces 73% of visitors.**
93 sessions (2nd highest on the site), 26.9% engagement, no apparent CTA.
This is likely organic search traffic landing on a content page and leaving.
Zero Chore Wheel installs or signups are being captured from the site's second-largest traffic source.

**Housing CTA rate from `/houses/sage` is ~9%.**
81 high-intent sessions → 7 total housing actions (4 `cta_waitlist_sage` + 3 `cta_interest_form`).
The bottleneck per `spec/HOUSING.md` is interest-to-application; the page is not converting the intent it already earns.

**Event name fragmentation breaks Chore Wheel conversion reporting.**
`cta_chorewheel_get_started` (12 events, **not in `utils/track.js`**) fires 3× more often than `cta_cw_get_started` (4 events, the canonical name in track.js).
A stale call site is splitting reported conversions.
The same problem affects `cta_email_chorewheel` (3 events, not in track.js vs. `cta_email_cw`), `cta_interest_form` (3 events, not in track.js), and `cta_book_consultation` (1 event) vs. `cta_book_consult` (track.js name).

**Direct traffic engagement is low (42.5%).**
Direct is the largest channel (219 sessions) but has the worst engagement rate.
This may be return visitors checking for updates or bot traffic, but it also suggests the homepage isn't holding attention for cold arrivals.

## Recommendations

### 1. Fix stale event names in track.js callers — **`/chorewheel`, `/about`** | Effort: S | Impact: High (data quality)

**The problem:** `cta_chorewheel_get_started` (12 fires) is a legacy event name still being called somewhere.
`utils/track.js` defines `cta_cw_get_started` — find the call site firing the old string and update it to `CTA.cwGetStarted`.
Also audit for `cta_email_chorewheel` → `CTA.emailCw`, `cta_interest_form` (no track.js constant — add one or alias to existing), and `cta_book_consultation` → `CTA.bookConsult`.
Until this is fixed, conversion reporting for the Chore Wheel funnel is structurally undercounted.

### 2. Add a visible interest CTA block to `/houses/sage` — Effort: S | Impact: High (housing pipeline)

**The problem:** 81 sessions at 244s avg — visitors are reading, not converting.
Only 4 `cta_waitlist_sage` events from those 81 visits.
Add a sticky or mid-page block: **"Interested in living at Sage House? Join the waitlist →"** linking to `/interest`.
Per `spec/HOUSING.md`, the reservoir/waitlist model depends on continuous interest capture; the page should push every engaged visitor toward the Tally form.
The event to fire is `CTA.waitlistSage` (`cta_waitlist_sage`).

### 3. Add a Chore Wheel CTA to `/p/chores-as-complex-coordination` — Effort: S | Impact: Medium (CW acquisition)

**The problem:** 93 sessions, 26.9% engagement, zero Chore Wheel installs captured.
This page is the site's second-largest traffic source, almost certainly organic search on "chores coordination" topics.
Add a bottom-of-post CTA block: **"Run a shared house? Chore Wheel handles this automatically. →"** pointing to `/chorewheel`.
Fire `CTA.cwGetStarted` (`cta_cw_get_started`) on click.
A secondary option: add a `newsletter_signup` CTA for readers not ready to install.

### 4. Add a "How applying works" 3-step block to `/houses/sage` — Effort: S | Impact: Medium (housing funnel)

**The problem:** Per `spec/HOUSING.md`, the bottleneck is between interest and application.
The page earns high dwell time but doesn't demystify next steps.
Add: **Apply → Visit → Move in** with one sentence per step and the 48-hour response commitment.
This is the June Homes pattern from `spec/GROWTH.md`: a concrete time commitment ("we respond within 48 hours") reduces perceived risk more than any copy.
Pair with the interest CTA block from rec #2.

### 5. Surface the `newsletter_signup` CTA on `/houses/sage` and `/chorewheel` — Effort: S | Impact: Medium (retention)

**The problem:** Newsletter signup (15 events) is the highest-converting action on the site but is only present on the homepage.
Both /houses/sage (housing intent) and /chorewheel (tool intent) are high-engagement pages with no newsletter capture.
Per `spec/GROWTH.md`, Substack is already the strongest distribution channel.
Add a low-friction email capture ("Get updates from Zaratan →") on both pages, firing `CTA.newsletterSignup` (`newsletter_signup`).
This creates a durable re-engagement path for the ~91% of /houses/sage visitors who don't convert to the waitlist in a single visit.
