# Zaratan Housing — Product Spec

Living product definition.
Last updated: 2026-03-29.

## What It Is

Zaratan creates naturally-affordable coliving by converting beautiful old buildings into high-density shared houses, operated dynamically with Chore Wheel.

Currently: Sage House (9 bedrooms, Highland Park, LA) and Cactus Cottage (studio, same lot).

## What We're Selling

**The hook (community):** Live with people who actually care about sharing a home.
Not a co-living brand with programming directors and forced social events.
A real community that runs itself, where residents have agency and contributions are valued.

**The close (value):** Beautiful 1905 Craftsman. $1,250/mo all-in (rent, utilities, food staples, internet, supplies). Below market for the quality and location.

**The differentiator (systems):** Chore Wheel is the mechanism that makes the community actually function.
Residents don't need a house manager telling them what to do. The system handles coordination, accountability, and institutional knowledge.

## Who It's For

People in LA looking for housing who want more than a room.
Typically: young professionals, creatives, students, people new to the city who want instant community.

**Not for:** People who want to live alone. People who want a hotel-style coliving experience with concierge services.

## The Funnel

1. **Discovery:** Substack blog, Craigslist listings, Instagram, word-of-mouth, website
2. **Interest capture:** Tally form at zaratan.world/interest → Airtable pipeline
3. **Application:** Full rental application (when vacancy exists)
4. **Move-in:** 80% of applicants who complete the application convert

The bottleneck is between interest and application.
The reservoir/waitlist model (capture interest continuously, activate when vacancy occurs) addresses this.

## Vacancy Model

- 9 residents, 2-3 vacancies per year
- ~2/3 historically filled via referrals, ~1/3 via listings
- Goal: reduce dependence on referrals by building a reliable non-referral pipeline

## What Residents Experience

- A well-maintained, aesthetically beautiful house
- Shared spaces: restaurant-style kitchen, dining room, living room, art space, upstairs deck, backyard fire pit, recording studio, gym, guest room
- Chore Wheel handling task coordination and accountability
- A self-directed community with minimal management oversight
- All-inclusive pricing (no surprise utility bills, no supply runs)

## What Success Looks Like

- Vacancies fill within 2 weeks of posting
- Residents stay 12+ months on average
- The house functions without founder involvement in daily operations
- Resident satisfaction: "best living situation I've ever had"
